Create Derived Variables

In assessing both "purchase behavior" and "basis for purchase", there are categories in each which are too broad to use for analysis (Br.Cd. & PropCat 5-15). Because K Means does not work with categorical variables, I will generate a derived variable (as per the recommendation in the assignment) for Br.Cd, and PropCat 5-15. This simply specifies that the customer has a high affinity toward one of the specific categories or brands.

Cluster Analysis

Cluster 0 - 'Brand Champions'

These members tend to gravitate toward one brand (High Top Brand % vs. low No. of Brands and Others 999). This member is not as responsive to promotion (high Pur Vol No Promo - %). Responsive to specific props and price categories (high PropCat 14, Top PropCat% and Pr Cat 3.

Cluster 1 - 'Affluent Movers'

This member spends a lot overall (High Value, High Total Volume) and has a propensity for affluence (high Affluence Index). This member skews older and female (AGE, SEX), with others in the household (HS). While this member likes to shop multiple brands, (high Others 999, low Top Brand %), they tend to shop a lot before moving to the next brand (high Brand Run).

Cluster 2 - 'Thrifty Nomads'

This member is responsive to promotions (Pur Vol Promo 6 %, PropCat 15, PropCat 8). High likelihood to shop multiple brands (high Others 999, low Top Brand %).
